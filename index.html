SECTION 01

About Me

Mechanical Engineering student at UCL (expected graduation June 2028), specialising in robotics, control systems, and mechatronic design. I have a passion for solving complex, multidisciplinary engineering problems at the confluence of hardware, software, and physics.

My skillset at a glance

My technical expertise includes:

  • High-level control algorithm design in MATLAB/Simulink
  • Low-level FPGA-based signal processing
  • Embedded C++ firmware (ESP32/Arduino)
  • Custom PCB design and PCB assembly
  • Mechanical CAD modelling (Fusion 360) optimised for manufacturing and assembly (DfMA)

However, working on each aspect individually is only half the story. My strongest trait is my ability to work on and integrate several of the above aspects into a single cohesive system. To me, modern engineering is too integrated and cross-functional to only focus on a single aspect, the best solutions come from those who can understand and coordinate across software, electronics and mechanical components.

What I can bring to your team

I am an engineer who works best in fast-paced, dynamic environments. I flourish when I am working on complex cross-functional projects and teams such as those in a start-up.

When it comes to learning, I always want to know the 'why': Why things work and more importantly, why they don't. Learning the basis of concepts and how they relate to previous knowledge has helped me to solve problems faster and discover solutions I wouldn't have found otherwise. I am also someone who thoroughly enjoys communicating complex technical ideas intuitively and concisely to audiences, whether it be fellow engineers or even non-technical stakeholders.

The attributes above, combined with my experience and passion for merging mechanical, electrical and software engineering principles, is what led me to the fields of robotics, mechatronics and control systems. These are fields in which engineers, regardless of their role, must be capable of communicating across disciplines and integrating their work across all three of the aforementioned engineering fields.

Systems Engineering Intern

Leonardo UK

Summer 2025 & 2026
