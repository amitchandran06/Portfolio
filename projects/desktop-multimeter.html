Electronics Prototyping Station

A-level Project (2023/24) — A comprehensive user-oriented design, integrating an Arduino MEGA, custom PCB, and 3D printed DFMA enclosure into an all-in-one testing station.

End-to-end Design

01 — THE BRIEF

This was an open design project, with the only goals being to develop a unique product, that would solve a real-world problem for a tangible target market and end user. Much of early stages of the project was spent analysing the market and identifying a problem that could be solved within the scope of the time and budget.

02 — DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

Being an avid hobbyist in DIY electronics, one of the first things that came to mind was just how complex and uninuitive the process of DIYing can be for beginners. It was hardly a welcoming space, and with IoT and consumer AI taking off, it was a time where DIY IoT was gaining more traction than ever. This led me to developing what I called : The Electronics Prototyping Station. An 'All-in-one' product that would allow user to easily test and develop their own hobby level circuits, aiming at the very avid but amateur audience.

03 — TECHNICAL DEPTH & ACHIEVEMENTS
  • Custom PCB Architecture: The development of the electornics followed a systems engineering 'V' model. With subsystems (each measurement mode) being developed individually and verified. Then after fine tuning each sub-system, I moved onto integrating each one into one unified system. This would involve converting breadboard designs into a PCB layout
  • Onboard UI: Having been powered by a microcontroller, the onboard UI was very simple. It feature a 128x128 1.8inch TFT display. This allowed for simple but intutive UIs for each mode, including a graphing mode for voltage which I developed from scratch (see PDF for details). Modes were changed via the use of simple SPST buttons, which then triggered hardware interrupts in the program, as to not disrupt the logic.
  • Autoranging Measurements One of the best feature of this product was its auto-ranging feature. Unlike many entry level multimeters, my product was capable automatically adjusting the measurement range. Allowing for accurate readings across 3-9 orders of magnitude depending on the mode. This included measurements of capactiance, which most entry-level multimeters omit completely. The circuit made use of the exponential curve that capacitor charge sequences follow, allowing the program to choose different resistors to charge through. This ensured the charge time for the capacitor was in the range of maximum resolution for the Arduino, providing more accurate results
  • DfMA Principles and User-friendliness: One of the core principles for the design was for it to be beginner friendly. This meant ensuring that assembly was intutive and did not require advanced tools. The operation of the product was also to be simple, using minimal proprietary components as possible. To ensure this, I designed the enclosure to only feature one screw size, and slots where possible. This allowed for the station to be highly modular, with components easily able to be hot-swapped to specific needs. The station also featured USB ports for powering your own circuits, as well as USB C power to be as universal as possible.
SKILLS USED: C++ EasyEDA Fusion 360 PCB Assembly & Soldering 3D Printing / Rapid Manufacturing Workshop Equipment
04 — OUTCOME

Delivered a fully functional, high-accuracy desktop testing station with accurate measurement capabilites for : Voltage, Resistance , Capacitance, Current and Continuity. Succesfully satisfied vast majority end user requirements, scoring 100/100.
