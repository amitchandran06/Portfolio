PROJECT 003 · MECHANICAL & HARDWARE DESIGN

Analog Robotic Device

IMechE Design Challenge (2025) — Autonomous robotic device with a custom PCB and generatively designed chassis. Featuring an electronics solution with no micrcontroller, all logic is derived from the hardware components.

ROLE
Electro-Mechanical Lead
TIMELINE
2025
STACK
EasyEDA · Fusion 360
OUTCOME
3rd Place - Most Unique Design (45 Teams)
01 — THE BRIEF

The brief for this challenge was strictly defined, which is very different from the open nature of many of my other project briefs. This gave me a much clearer understanding of how to design a product to very strict requirements. The specifics of the regulations are detailed in the PDFs below. However, the core aspect of the challenge was to develop a robotic device that could move perfectly linearily, from a start point, to a variable distane target, then return to exactly where it started. This had to be achieved without any programmable logic.

02 — DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

One of the core aspects of our design philosophy was to create a sleek, easily assembleable and most importantly, a reusable design. Out of all of my projects, this was the one which involved the most iterative testing. Electronics were iterated and changed dozens of times, even navigating through last minute regulation changes. The design would follow a very organic process. With wiring, placement and mechanical design all seamlessly confluencing.

Testing for electronics followed the general process of breadboarding, verifying results, verifying compatibility with mechanism, and finally PCB production and assembly. Use of JST connectors were prioritsed over soldering to allow for easy component replacement and testing.

03 — TECHNICAL DEPTH & ACHIEVEMENTS
  • Circuit Simulation & Debugging: Circuitry was designed on EasyEDA, with simulations conducted on an online platform called Falstad. The design of the circuit had to be strictly compliant with regulations. To avoid using programmable microcontrollers, the circuit was designed with discrete logic gates to trigger waiting sequences and shut down. Custom H-bridge circuits were implemented to allow for automatic reversal of the motor direction electronically. This was unique amongst competition , providing us with a more solid state and reliable system
  • PCB Layout & Weight Optimisation: Optimisation and assembly were priorities for our design. The cost cap for the project was strict, meaning components and frame weight had to be tightly controlled. This prompted us to develop an organic, generatively designed frame. Which in turn required wire lengths and layouts to be very carefully chosen
  • Mechanical Timer The competition required the device to wait for 10-15 seconds once at the wall. Initally, we opted for a solution based off of an RC circuit. However, a last minute regulation change required a rethink. We eventually re-designed the timer mechanically. Utilising fixed speed rotation to trigger an SPDT switch. However, the most unique aspect of our design was was the ability for our timer to automatically reset after each run. Enabling a level of autonomy other designs did not have.
SKILLS USED: EasyEDA Fusion 360 PCB Assembly & Soldering
04 — OUTCOME

Produced a fully functional design scoring 99/100 in the final competition. The design was the most unique out of all 45 teams present, being the only one to feature a custom PCB and automatic timer reset.
