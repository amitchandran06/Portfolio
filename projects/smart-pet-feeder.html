IoT Integrated Pet Feeder

01 — THE BRIEF

The brief of this project was to develop an open-source product for a distinct target market. The product must also focus on a being part of the 'circular economy' principle, ensuring recylability and repairability. As a group, we settled on pet feeding as our context, the problem statement is defined in the PDF below.

02 — DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

The design philosphy was centered around a very intuitive and open-source design process. Every component and layout was chosen with the intent of the end user either producing it or assembling it themselves (see assembly manual PDF). This prompted us to opt for a breadboard design rather than a PCB due to it being more practical for users to build.

The software and firmware were also developed in a totally open-source fashion. With all code available on GitHub, allowing for user to adjust parameters as appropriate. Another key aspect to the pet-feeder was use of no onboard controls or inbuilt screens, instead making use of the user's mobile phone to program meals. This minimises the e-waste produced.

03 — TECHNICAL DEPTH & ACHIEVEMENTS
  • Electronics Architecture: The electronics for the pet feeder is centralised around an ESP32-S3 microcontroller. The ESP32 is responsible for controlling / interfacing with a NEMA17 stepper motor, as well as HX711 strain gauge amplifer. My role focused on programming the ESP32, allowing for precise mass measuring as well as accurate portion control
  • Event-Driven BLE Communication: The user interfaces with the pet feeder settings via BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy) on an Android client. Users can program specific meal times and masses for their pets. Communication was handled on the ESP32 side via a GATT server using the Nordic UART Service (NUS) with event-driven callback handlers (onWrite, onConnect, onSubscribe) for lowest latencies.
  • RTC Syncing: Instead of adding a dedicated RTC unit, we opted to sync the onboard ESP32 clock with real time whenever a bluetooth message is tramsitted from the phone. This saved money and space and makes use of exisitng hardware (the user's phone) instead of introducing new components
  • Power Distrubtion: Since the product usecase was very dynamic, it was important that the product could take in a range of power supplies. To accomodate this, I developed an intutive and logical power distrubtion layout using buck-boosts. This set up made assembly simple and also made it easier to debug systems by isolating different power levels (Motor and ESP32).
SKILLS USED: C++ EasyEDA PCB Assembly & Soldering
04 — OUTCOME

We produced a fully functional prototype, with a functional Android app client companion. Portion control was accurate up to 2% of masses in range 20-100g. Timing was accurate with a maximum delay of 2 seconds with minimal drift. The product was tested using real pet food substitutes (skittles!).
